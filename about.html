ABOUT ME

As of May 2024, I am a graduate research assistant at Purdue University pursuing a PhD in forestry. Previously, I was the center lead at the Goddard Space Flight Center in Maryland for the NASA DEVELOP National Program. I supervised teams and developed projects using NASA Earth observations to address environmental issues. Before becoming the GSFC center lead, I participated for two terms virtually at the CO node in the Spring of 2023 on an agriculture project assessing the drivers of production on the Western Slopes of the Rockies and at the PUP node in the Summer of 2023 on an ecological forecasting project assessing avoided soil carbon loss via conservation easements in New York.

I graduated from Indiana University with a Bachelor of Arts in Psychology and minors in Counseling and Philosophy of Mind and Cognition in 2017. In 2019, I pursued my passion for forestry at the University of Maine, where I earned both a Master of Forestry and a Master of Science in Forest Resources.
