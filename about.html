ABOUT ME

I am currently a graduate research assistant at Purdue University, working towards a PhD in forestry. Previously, I served as the center lead at the NASA DEVELOP National Program located at the Goddard Space Flight Center in Maryland. In this role, I oversaw teams and spearheaded projects utilizing NASA Earth observations to address environmental challenges.Before assuming the position of center lead, I participated in two terms as a virtual team member. In the Spring of 2023, I contributed to an agriculture project at the CO node, focusing on analyzing production drivers in the Western Slopes of the Southern Rockies. Subsequently, during the Summer of 2023, I collaborated on an ecological forecasting project at the PUP node, evaluating the impact of conservation easements on soil carbon preservation in New York.

I earned my Bachelor of Arts in Psychology from Indiana University in 2017, with minors in Counseling and Philosophy of Mind and Cognition. Building on my academic foundation, I pursued my passion for forestry at the University of Maine, where I obtained both a Master of Forestry and a Master of Science in Forest Resources in 2021 and 2023, respectively.
